Checkerboard Cipher

Description

A mixed alphabet is is generated and used to fill a table. The first column and the first line contain the symbols that will be used to represent the plain text letters:
0 1 2 3 4
0 E N T R H
1 K Y A B C
2 D F G I L
3 M O P Q S
4 U V W X Z
To encipher, a letter of the plain text is represented by the labels in the first column and first row, e. g. N is enciphered as 01 and O is enciphered as 31. Beside a key word and alphabet you may choose how the mixed alphabet is generated:

The cipher can't conserve the capitalization or text structure. So far, only numbers 0 to 4(5) are used for the output. It does only accept alphabets with 25 (=5 x 5) or 36 (=6 x 6) letters. Future versions of CipherClerk's Applet may offer more possibilities for this.
